NAL - Native American Languages collection at Sam Noble Museum
Choices:
Level 1. Open access - Users have full access to these materials after agreeing to our Conditions for Use of Archived Resources. For this level, we assume that depositors have already gained permission for public access from the speakers or authors of the resource.
Level 2. Materials are available to view onsite but no copies may distributed. - Users have full access to these materials in our reading room at the Sam Noble Museum but will not be issued copies.
Level 3. Access is protected by a time limit. - When you deposit with the archive, you will specify a date. After that date, access will change to Level 1. This option is good for resources that are only sensitive for a period of time; for example, the lifetime of the narrator or the five years until you finish your thesis. You will still be able to access these materials yourself with the password.
Level 4. The depositor (or someone else) controls access to the resource. - NAL will provide contact information and the user must contact the access controller directly for permission. If permission is granted, the controller will inform NAL and NAL will allow the requestor access to the resource. The controller must ensure that the appropriate contact information is up to date. If contact information is not up to date, then determinations of permission to access and use the resource revert to the curator of NAL.
